PORTFOLIO-WIDE SAFETY INCIDENT SUMMARY
Reporting period: January 1, 2025 — March 31, 2026 (15 months)
Prepared by: Corporate Safety Director
Document ID: SAF-SUM-2026-Q1
Distribution: Executive Committee, Project Executives, Insurance Carrier

1. OVERVIEW

This summary covers all OSHA-recordable safety incidents across the active project portfolio (15 projects, $725M aggregate contract value) for the trailing 15-month period. A total of 32 recordable incidents were logged: 26 first-aid only, 4 lost-time injuries (LTI), 1 hospitalization, and 1 fatality. The portfolio total recordable incident rate (TRIR) for the period was 2.1, slightly below the BLS construction-industry average of 2.7 but above the company strategic target of 1.8.

The fatality, occurring at the Airport Terminal Expansion project on August 12, 2025, has been investigated by OSHA, the company's internal safety review board, and an independent third-party safety consultant. Findings are summarized in section 4.

2. INCIDENT BREAKDOWN BY PROJECT

Airport Terminal Expansion (Northeast, $120M): 9 recordable incidents — 7 first-aid, 1 LTI, 1 fatality. The highest incident count in the portfolio. Contributing factors: scale (peak workforce of 340), aggressive schedule, multiple trades on the same floor plate, and complex airside coordination requirements.

Wastewater Treatment Plant (Southeast, $88M): 6 recordable incidents — 5 first-aid, 1 LTI. Heavy mechanical and confined-space work; two of the first-aid incidents involved minor chemical exposure during testing.

University Science Building (Northeast, $52M): 4 recordable incidents — all first-aid. Predominantly slips/trips during winter months; site-wide ice management program revised in November 2025.

Hospital Renovation (Midwest, $35M): 3 recordable incidents — 2 first-aid, 1 LTI. The LTI involved a rigger struck by a swinging beam on June 4, 2025; rigger returned to work after 18 days. Lift planning protocols were updated portfolio-wide in response.

Coastal Highway Rehab (Southwest, $67M, in progress): 3 recordable incidents — 3 first-aid. All three involved highway-traffic interface; project has since deployed a dedicated flagging crew and added a temporary concrete barrier.

Harbor Bridge Expansion (Northeast, $45M): 2 recordable incidents — 2 first-aid.

Residential Tower Block A (Southeast, $18M): 2 recordable incidents — 2 first-aid.

Industrial Warehouse Complex, Solar Farm Installation, Wind Farm Phase 1, Data Center Build, Downtown Office Tower, Retail Shopping Center, Bridge Deck Replacement, Rail Yard Modernization: 1 incident or fewer each. Combined: 3 first-aid, 1 hospitalization (Solar Farm — heat stress event July 2025, worker recovered fully).

4. FATALITY INVESTIGATION SUMMARY

Date: August 12, 2025
Project: Airport Terminal Expansion
Trade: Steel erection
Description: An ironworker was struck by a falling steel decking sheet during deck installation at level 4. The deck sheet (approximately 180 lbs) had been temporarily laid in place but not yet welded down. A gust of wind, combined with the bundle straps having been removed prematurely by a separate crew, caused the sheet to slide off the supporting beam.

Root causes identified:
- Sequencing failure: deck strap removal was performed by a non-erection crew member without verification that the sheet was secured.
- Supervision gap: the lead foreman for steel erection was on a different floor at the time.
- Communication failure: the sequencing change had been discussed in the morning toolbox talk but not documented in the daily lift plan.

Corrective actions (deployed portfolio-wide by October 2025):
- All steel decking strap removal must be performed by the erection crew only, verified by signed sign-off on the daily deck plan.
- Daily lift plans now require a written sequencing diagram for any deck installation work, signed by the lead foreman and the site safety officer before work begins.
- Pre-task briefings are now mandatory before deck strap removal, regardless of crew familiarity.
- A wind-speed cutoff of 20 mph (sustained) for unsecured deck panels has been added to the company-wide steel-erection SOP.

OSHA citations issued: 2 serious citations, $28,420 total fine (paid). No willful or repeat citations. The independent third-party review concluded that the company's safety program design was adequate but that field execution of the lift-plan documentation requirement had been inconsistent — a finding the corrective actions directly address.

5. TRENDS

Comparing the trailing 15 months to the prior 15 months, total recordable incidents decreased from 41 to 32 (-22%), but severity increased — the prior period had no fatalities. First-aid incidents are down 26%; LTI rate is flat; hospitalization rate is up (0 → 1).

The largest year-over-year improvement is in slip/trip/fall incidents on completed projects, which decreased 38% following the Q4 2024 deployment of the standardized site housekeeping protocol.

6. RECOMMENDATIONS

- Maintain the wind-speed cutoff and lift-plan documentation enforcement on all active steel erection work.
- Expand the airside-coordination training program (originally developed for Airport Terminal) to all infrastructure projects with vehicular interface.
- Continue rolling out the standardized housekeeping protocol; uptake on the four newest projects is currently 60%.
- Bring TRIR back to the company target of 1.8 by year-end 2026 through continued focus on the top three incident types.

End of summary.
